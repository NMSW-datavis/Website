What This Project Is About

Our goal for this project was to take complex, often overwhelming data about global conflicts and turn it into something clear and interactive. We're using data from trusted sources (like ACLED and Our World in Data) to show the patterns behind these events, hoping to make the information more accessible to everyone, from students to researchers.

Project Goals

Our main objectives were to:

  • Turn confusing stats into clear, visual stories.
  • Make this data easy for anyone to access and understand.
  • Use data to show the real human side of these conflicts.
  • Provide a clear basis for policy and research.

Data Overview

The data we're visualizing includes:

  1. Global conflict events and locations
  2. Casualty statistics and trends over time
  3. Displacement and refugee data
  4. Humanitarian impact assessments
  5. Regional conflict intensity metrics

How We're Building It

Our approach uses modern HTML and CSS to build interactive visualizations. We focused on:

  • Temporal analysis (how conflicts change over time)
  • Geographic mapping of conflict zones
  • Comparative studies across regions
  • Quantifying the humanitarian impact

Where Our Data Comes From

We rely on high-quality, professional datasets to make sure our visualizations are accurate and meaningful.

ACLED

ACLED is our primary source for real-time event data. It's incredibly detailed and lets us see *who* was involved, *what* happened, and *where*.

Our World in Data

Our World in Data is perfect for seeing the 'big picture.' We use it to get historical context and understand long-term trends in conflict-related deaths.
